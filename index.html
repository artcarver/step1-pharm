Autonomic Pharmacology
Master receptor subtypes first (M1/M2/M3, α1/α2, β1/β2) and the drugs fall into place. High yield for organ specific side effects and clinical applications.
♥
Cardiovascular Pharmacology
The most tested drug system on Step 1. Know ACEi/ARBs cold. Antiarrhythmic classes: think ion channels first, then drugs. Diuretic electrolyte effects are guaranteed questions.
🩸
Blood, Coagulation & Rheumatology
Anticoagulants appear on nearly every Step 1 exam. Know reversal agents cold. Heparin induced thrombocytopenia (HIT) is a classic trap. NSAIDs and DMARDs are heavily tested for toxicities.
🧠
Neuro / Psychiatric Pharmacology
Memorize antidepressant side effect profiles (TCA overdose, SSRI serotonin syndrome). Antipsychotic EPS and NMS are classic vignettes. Antiepileptic drugs require exact mechanism knowledge.
🦠
Antimicrobial Pharmacology
Organized by mechanism. For each drug know its unique toxicity, not just its coverage. Antibiotic associated C. diff is a perennial vignette.
🔬
GI & Endocrine Pharmacology
Diabetes pharmacology is enormous. Know every class MOA, when to use it, and key side effects. Corticosteroids are the highest yield drug class for toxicity.
🫁
Pulmonary Pharmacology
Asthma step therapy is heavily tested. Know which drugs are for acute relief vs. maintenance. Antihistamine receptor subtypes determine organ effects and drug indication.
⚗
Antineoplastic Pharmacology
Step 1 tests toxicities more than mechanisms. Key rule: every chemo drug has one unique, memorable toxicity. Learn these cold.
